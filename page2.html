Places To Visit!

Prague Castle

  • The largest ancient castle in the world: Area of 750,000 sq ft, length of 1,870 ft, and width of 430 ft.
  • A castle complex built in the 9th century.
  • The official office of the current President of the Czech Republic
  • Was the seat of power for kings of Bohemia, Holy Roman emperors, and former presidents of Czechoslovakia
  • Houses the Bohemian Crown Jewels

Charles Bridge

  • Most important connection between Prague Castle and the Prague's Old Town and adjacent areas
  • Crosses the Vltava river; Construction began in 1357 under King Charles IV and finished in early 15th century
  • 1,693 ft long and 33 ft wide, 16 ice guarded protected arches and 3 bridge towers; 2 on the Lesser Quarter side, Malá Strana Bridge Tower, and 1 on the Old Town side, Old Town Bridge Tower
  • Decorated with 30 replica baroque statues of saints and patron saints famous during the 17th-18th century originals are exhibited in the Lapidarium of the National Museum.
  • Land connection made Prague important trade route between Eastern and Western Europe
  • Replaced the Judith Bridge (1158-1172) that was damaged by a flood in 1342
  • Has been referred as "Charles Bridge" since 1870; Originally called "Stone Bridge" (Kamenný most) or "Prague Bridge"(Pražský most)

Old Town Square (Staromák)

  • The historic square in the Old Town quarter of Prague
  • Located between Wenceslas Saqure and Charles Bridge
  • Buildings included in the square:
    • The Gothic Church of Church of Our Lady before Týn - main church of this city's part since 14th century; Towers are 80 m high
    • The Baroque St. Nicholas Chuch
    • Prague Orloj (Astronomical Clock) – the medieval astronoical clock mounted on the Old Town Hall; 3rd oldest clock in the world and the oldest clock in operation
    • Kinský Palace - an art museum of the Czech National Gallery
